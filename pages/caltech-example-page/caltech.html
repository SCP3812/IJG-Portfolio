Caltech Research Internship Presentation

Presentation regarding my findings of how change in gut bacteria modulates ornithine levels in fruit flies

Background

Last summer, I had the fantastic opportunity to do an internship with Caltech at one of their biological research laboratories. I was ecstatic about starting it; it would be the first time I would have ever stepped into a wet lab (not including the labs at my school, of course). On the actual in-person first day, the Grad student I was working under had begun to show me the hoops of the lab, the autoclave, the disinfectant procedures, the difference between different growth solutions, etc., and I was also shown the quirk of this particular lab; working with fruit flies.






Experience

Aside from being an interesting parallel to human life, these flies were part of my work in the lab. Specifically, I was assigned to perform an experiment seeking to test the effects of bacteria harvested from yogurt on the flies ornithine levels (a specific amino acid, associated with protein formation).

This experiment involved growing the yogurt bacteria themselves in a specialized growth medium, then purifying the bacterial sludge and feeding it to the flies, then homogenizing the flies and testing the ornithine levels with a specialized assay.


I finally put my findings into a presentation, and presented it to a fairly large audience towards the end of my internship tenure.
